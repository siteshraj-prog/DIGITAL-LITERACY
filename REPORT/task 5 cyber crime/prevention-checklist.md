**Stay Safe On The Internet: Checklist For Student Safety**


1. UPI Payments Need A Code:When someone asks you for your UPI code while transferring money to your account via UPI, they are trying to scam you, don't ever provide it!
2. Double-Check The VPA: Before you click on "Pay" you should always verify that you have the right VPS or that the correct name appears on the scanner.
3. Audit App Permissions: Go through your apps and check which ones have Overlay or Screen record permissions, revoke those permissions on all non-essentials.
4. Use 2FA: Turn on two-factor authentication for all your user accounts (Email, Google, LinkedIn, GitHub).
5. Access Public Wi-Fi With Caution: Avoid logging into the banking app or VTOP over unsecured public wifi, try to use your cell phone / 4G services or VPN service instead.
6. Confirm The Website Url: Before entering your log in details, ensure that the website has an "https: //" or "http: // and correct spelling (ex. vitbhopal.ac.in not vit-bhopal-login.com).
7. Share Less Information: Do not post your Aadhar#, DOB & phone # on public Github repositories or on social media bios.
8. Report Fraud Immediately: If you feel that you are a victim of fraud, the following is how to report it through the proper channels.
    National Cyber Crime Reporting Centre; [suspicious link removed]
    National Reporting Centre Helpline: 1930.
