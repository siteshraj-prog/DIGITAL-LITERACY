During my research in UPI fraud, I found nothing more astonishing than how easy it is for an attacker to trick an individual through social engineering, which is far more potent than hacking. What is astonishing is that in most cases, victims are not really "hacked" through coding, but are tricked through psychological pressure or through being offered something in return, such as a "cashback." The idea of an individual being able to withdraw an entire bank account balance simply by being shown a screen-sharing session is perhaps one of the bigger wake-up calls.

One of the personal effects of this research is that I am now changing my behavior of using the same PIN for multiple applications. I have now implemented an entirely unique and complex PIN for my financial applications, which is completely different from my phone's screen lock. I am also being much more careful with regards to which applications I allow access to my screen through "Accessibility" and "Screen Overlay" permissions.
