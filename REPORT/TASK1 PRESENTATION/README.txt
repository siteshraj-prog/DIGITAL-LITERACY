For this task, I designed a simple infographic using Canva, focusing on the simplification of digital literacy for my peers. The infographic consists of three zones: a conceptual description of digital literacy, a "Digital Shield" section for internet safety, and a section for a professional online presence. I have used a high-contrast color combination of "Navy Blue" and "Electric Yellow" for this infographic, moving away from the "wall of text" that most academic resources contain. 
One of the most interesting aspects of this task for me was the "Grandma Rule" for digital safety. In today's digital world, most of us are so focused on password security that the most vulnerable part of our online presence is often the information we are willing to share. The most difficult part of this task for me was the curation of the content. Digital literacy is a broad field, and narrowing it down to only three aspects required aggressive editing to keep the content punchy. This task also taught me that being digitally literate is not only a skill, but a state of mind that requires curiosity.
