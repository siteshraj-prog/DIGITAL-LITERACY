One of the best examples of bad digital communication is when a student sends an informal email to a professor, such as: "hey prof, need more time thx." In an actual scenario, this would result in the student being denied his/her request. This would cause an unhealthy relationship between the student and professor, which would further harm the student's academic and professional reputation.

In order to avoid such an occurrence, it is imperative for the student to include his/her registration number in the email's subject line, which is 25BSA10110. Also, it is vital for him/her to include a formal greeting. This would turn an informal email into a professional email. These are some of the nuances we can learn here in VIT Bhopal, which would come in handy in our future digital interactions with global recruiters.
